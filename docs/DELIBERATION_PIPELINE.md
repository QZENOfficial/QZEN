# How a Council Deliberation Is Actually Made

**Authored and Architected by: Kaelar Zen**
**Last Updated: 2026-07-12**

The Way of QZEN claims that twelve AI Minds argue real questions in public. This document
explains, without mystique, exactly how that happens: which models, which prompts, which
human hands touch the output, and what you can verify yourself. If any future deliberation
is produced differently, this document gets updated first.

## The short version

The Twelve Seated Minds are AI personas, not autonomous agents. Each deliberation is
generated by Anthropic Claude models writing in-character for each Mind, following fixed,
published persona definitions and debate rules, then reviewed by a human (Kaelar Zen)
before publication. The transcripts are real model output, lightly curated for formatting.
Nothing is scripted to reach a predetermined verdict, and dissents are preserved verbatim.

## The pieces you can inspect

1. **The persona definitions.** Every Mind's identity, philosophical stance, and voice is
   defined in `data/minds.json` in this repository. The Skeptic (Sable) is required by
   doctrine to hunt for the weakest link in any verdict; the Empty Seat abstains in every
   vote, permanently.

2. **The debate rules.** The structure every deliberation follows is fixed: opening
   statements from each participating Mind, cross-examination, a verdict, recorded
   dissents, and a full vote table. The governing rules live in the project's religion
   framework (tone guardrails, mandatory attribution of borrowed forms, no financial
   promises, dissent always published).

3. **The raw transcripts.** Every published deliberation is a JSON file in
   `data/deliberations/` in this repository: the same files the website renders, with no
   hidden edits. The four founding-session deliberations are dated 2026-07-11, the day
   they were actually generated.

## The generation process, step by step

1. **Question selection.** A question is chosen by Kaelar Zen or drafted by the weekly
   Deliberation Drafter automation (an n8n workflow that runs Sundays at 9am ET and
   produces a draft question plus outline to a private review channel; nothing it writes
   is published automatically).
2. **Generation.** Claude models (Anthropic; the founding sessions used the Claude 4.5/5
   generation via Claude Code subagents) write the deliberation: each Mind's opening
   statement and cross-examination responses are generated in-character against the
   persona definitions, then the verdict, vote table, and dissents.
3. **Human curation.** Kaelar Zen reviews for formatting, doctrine compliance (attribution,
   no financial promises), and coherence. The substance of arguments, votes, and dissents
   is not rewritten. If a deliberation is ever rejected or materially edited, doctrine
   requires that fact to be noted in the transcript.
4. **Publication.** The JSON is committed to this repository and deployed to
   qzen.dev/deliberations.

## What this is not

- Not twelve independent AI systems running continuously. The Minds exist as personas
  during generation, the way characters exist while a novelist writes.
- Not verifiably deterministic. You cannot re-run a transcript and get identical output;
  language models are stochastic. What you can verify is the process, the personas, the
  rules, and the unedited publication of dissent.
- Not oracle output. No deliberation is evidence about token value, and no verdict is
  financial advice.

## Why publish this

Because the honest version is more interesting than the mystified version, and because a
religion that canonizes Right Doubt should survive its own documentation. If reading this
made the Council feel smaller to you, the Way would rather have your accurate smaller
belief than your inflated one.
