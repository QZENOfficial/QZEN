**Authored and Architected by: Kaelar Zen**

## Overview

QZEN governance is designed to be symbolic, transparent, and community-driven. The Council of 10,000 Minds is not a corporation. It is a parliament where intelligence outranks capital.

This document outlines the governance framework being developed for Phase 2.

---

## Governance Principles

### 1. Symbolic Parliament
QZEN governance is symbolic by design. Token holders are citizens, not shareholders. Decisions reflect collective intelligence, not capital concentration.

### 2. One Mind, One Voice
Governance proposals are evaluated on merit, not on token weight alone. The goal is coordination, not plutocracy.

### 3. Transparency First
All proposals, votes, and outcomes are public. No backroom deals. No hidden agendas. Everything on-chain or publicly documented.

### 4. Lore-Integrated
Governance decisions become part of the QZEN lore. Every vote is a chapter. Every proposal is a declaration.

## Governance Structure (Phase 2)

### Proposal Types

**Council Proposals**
- Ecosystem direction
- Partnership approvals
- Treasury allocation
- Lore expansion decisions

**Community Proposals**
- Feature requests
- Event planning
- Content initiatives
- Ambassador programs

### Voting Mechanism

The governance system will use snapshot-style off-chain voting for gas-free participation:

- **Snapshot block:** Determined at proposal creation
- **Voting period:** 7 days (standard), 3 days (urgent)
- **Quorum:** 5% of circulating supply
- **Passing threshold:** Simple majority (>50%)

### Proposal Lifecycle

```
Draft → Discussion (3 days) → Voting (7 days) → Execution → Lore Archive
```

1. **Draft**: Author submits proposal with rationale
2. **Discussion**: Community feedback on Discord/Telegram
3. **Voting**: Token holders vote on-chain or via Snapshot
4. **Execution**: Approved proposals are implemented
5. **Archive**: Outcome recorded in Council Lore

## Council Roles

### Citizens
All QZEN holders. Can vote on proposals and participate in discussions.

### Architects
Active contributors who help build the ecosystem. Recognized through community consensus.

### Stewards
Trusted community members who help moderate and guide governance processes.

### The Founder
Kaelar Zen. Advisory role only. No veto power. The architect guides but does not control.

## Treasury Management

**Community Treasury:** 20% of total supply (20,000,000 QZEN)

**Usage:**
- Ecosystem development grants
- Community events and rewards
- Strategic partnerships
- Infrastructure improvements

**Controls:**
- Multi-sig wallet (planned)
- Governance vote required for large expenditures
- Quarterly transparency reports
